---

## Phase 1: Pre-Battle Preparation

### Company Deep Dive
- [ ] Re-read job description
- [ ] Study company website (mission, values, recent news)
- [ ] Check their LinkedIn/Twitter for recent posts
- [ ] Research interviewers on LinkedIn
- [ ] Identify 3 thoughtful questions to ask them

### Technical Prep (if applicable)
- [ ] Review key technologies listed in job description
- [ ] Practice coding challenges (LeetCode/HackerRank)
- [ ] Review portfolio projects to discuss
- [ ] Prepare to explain past work clearly

### Behavioral Prep
- [ ] Review STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
- [ ] Prepare 5 stories about past achievements
- [ ] Practice "Tell me about yourself" pitch
- [ ] Practice "Why this company?" answer
- [ ] Practice "Why are you leaving current role?" (if applicable)

### Mock Interview
- [ ] Do 1 mock interview with friend/family
- [ ] Record yourself answering common questions
- [ ] Watch recording and identify areas to improve

---

## Phase 2: Gear Check (Day Before)

- [ ] Choose professional outfit
- [ ] Test video/audio setup (if virtual)
- [ ] Charge devices (laptop, phone, headphones)
- [ ] Print extra copies of resume
- [ ] Prepare notebook and pen for notes
- [ ] Get good sleep (no all-nighters!)

---

## Phase 3: The Battle (Interview Day)

### Pre-Interview
- [ ] Arrive 10 min early (or log on 5 min early for virtual)
- [ ] Quick breathing exercises (calm nerves)
- [ ] Review your prepared questions

### During Interview
- [ ] Greet interviewer warmly
- [ ] Listen actively, take notes
- [ ] Answer questions using STAR method
- [ ] Ask your prepared questions
- [ ] Show enthusiasm for the role
- [ ] Clarify next steps before ending

### Post-Interview (Within 2 hours)
- [ ] Write down everything you remember
- [ ] Note questions they asked
- [ ] Note what went well / what to improve
- [ ] Draft thank-you email

---

## Phase 4: Post-Battle Follow-Up

- [ ] Send thank-you email within 24 hours
- [ ] Connect with interviewer on LinkedIn
- [ ] Set reminder to follow up in 1 week (if no response)
- [ ] Update application tracker with interview notes
- [ ] Reflect in job search journal

---

## After the Outcome

### If Offer Received
- [ ] Celebrate! (You earned this, adventurer!)
- [ ] Review offer details carefully
- [ ] Negotiate if appropriate (salary, benefits, start date)
- [ ] Make decision within reasonable timeframe
- [ ] Notify other companies you're withdrawing

### If Rejected
- [ ] Allow yourself to feel disappointed (it's okay)
- [ ] Request feedback if possible
- [ ] Update your "Lessons Learned" document
- [ ] Apply improvements to next interview
- [ ] Remember: Every "no" gets you closer to "yes"
